## Brand & Style

Dopamine Anime is built for cheerful discovery and fan energy. It should feel like a premium digital toy: glossy, bouncy, and impossible to ignore.

## Colors

Electric pink is the lead signal for calls to action. Neon cyan and sunny yellow exist to create delight, categorization, and reward without turning the UI muddy.

## Typography

The pack uses one rounded geometric family to keep everything coherent and friendly. Headlines should feel chunky and sticker-like, while labels read as bright interface decals.

## Layout & Spacing

Spacing should be generous enough to let saturated accents breathe. Modules need clear separation so the interface stays playful instead of noisy.

## Elevation & Depth

Depth comes from soft glows and frosted surfaces rather than gray drop shadows. Higher layers should feel brighter and more tactile, like translucent plastic.

## Shapes

The shape language is deliberately extra-rounded. Cards, posters, inputs, and buttons should all feel soft and approachable, with pill shapes used frequently for interaction-heavy elements.

## Motion

Motion can be springier than in a tool-oriented UI, but it still needs discipline. Use bounce and glow as a feedback language, not as permanent decoration.

## Components

Primary buttons should be candy-bright and clearly dominant. Cards can use white or translucent surfaces, and bottom navigation should float as a glass object above content.

## Accessibility

Bright surfaces still need strict text contrast. Cyan focus indicators and pink active states must remain distinguishable for users who do not rely on color alone.

## Do / Don't

Do push saturation, roundness, and playful glow. Do not introduce hard black dividers, sharp corners, or overly dense information layouts.

A. AI App Shell

Feels most like a modern AI product. The chat flow owns the screen and the other pages behave like supporting destinations.

Pros

  • Strong AI-first identity
  • Fastest to understand for new users
  • Easy to market and evolve

Cons

  • Knowledge and design can feel secondary
  • Less room for dense desktop workflows

B. AI-First Multi-Page Shell

AI is still the home and emotional center, but Knowledge, Sketch, and Design remain real first-class pages with shared navigation and artifact handoff.

Pros

  • Best fit for your four-page scope
  • Keeps AI primary without collapsing everything into chat
  • Supports web and desktop equally well

Cons

  • Needs stronger information architecture work
  • More migration effort than option A

C. Creative Agent Studio

Leans into sketching and design generation. The chat stays important, but shares attention with a large visual stage.

Pros

  • Great for visual creation workflows
  • Strong identity for sketch/design users

Cons

  • Conflicts with your “conversation is the center” choice
  • Knowledge becomes harder to anchor

My recommendation is B: it best matches your current direction of AI-first, four pages only, and a real product shell rather than a single giant chat screen.
